Miles Davis Timeline
  • Born Miles Dewey Davis, III in Alton, Illinois on May 26, 1926 into an affluent family; his father was a dentist.
  • Age 13, Miles took trumpet lessons from musician, Elwood Buchanan, who stressed the importance of playing without vibrato. He played this way his whole career.
  • Age 18, Miles played for two weeks with Billy Eckstine’s band along with Dizzy Gillespie and Charlie “Yardbird” Parker.
  • After graduating High School, Miles moved to New York and studied music at the Juilliard School of Music.
  • Circa 1945, Miles replaces Dizzy Gillespie in the Charlie Parker Quintet.
  • 1948, Miles is at the forefront of the creation of the “Cool Jazz” movement.
  • 1949-50 recordings of the 9 member Jazz band featuring Miles Davis on trumpet would later become known as the collection, “Birth of the Cool,” which was released in ’56.
  • 1950, Miles begins a 4 year struggle with heroin addiction while recording phenomenal works with the likes of Thelonius Monk, Milt Jackson, Sonny Rollins. He also starts using the Harmon Mute on his trumpet, which will become his signature sound.
  • Circa ’54-’74, Miles is widely accepted as the most innovative musician in all of Jazz forming bands that feature John Coltrane, Cannonball Adderly, Red Garland, Bill Evans, to name a few.
  • Miles records albums in Hard Bop and Modal Jazz styles of playing.
  • Kind of Blue, in Modal Jazz style, sells 4 million copies and is deemed the most celebrated album in the history of Jazz.
  • Miles Davis died September 28, 1991 from the effects of a stroke, pneumonia and respiratory failure. He was 65 years old.
